What is India's projected GDP growth for the fiscal year 2024-2025?
Answer: India's GDP is projected to grow between 6.5% and 6.8% in the fiscal year 2024-2025. 


What factors are contributing to the expected strong domestic demand in the second half of the fiscal year?
Answer: The strong domestic demand is anticipated to be driven by a significant uptick in government spending. 

What is the projected GDP growth for the fiscal year 2025-2026?
Answer: The GDP growth for the fiscal year 2025-2026 is projected to be between 6.7% and 7.3%. 

What are the significant downside risks to India's economic growth outlook?
Answer: The significant downside risks include rising geopolitical uncertainties and potential disruptions to global trade and supply chains. 


How have geopolitical uncertainties affected India's economic projections?
Answer: Geopolitical uncertainties have led to a wider range in growth projections, indicating increased risks to the economic outlook. 

What impact could disruptions to global trade and supply chains have on India's economy?
Answer: Disruptions to global trade and supply chains could negatively affect demand for India's exports, thereby impacting overall economic growth. 

How does the current growth projection compare to previous estimates?
Answer: The current growth projection is lower than previously estimated, reflecting a slower first half of the fiscal year. 

What role does government spending play in India's economic outlook?
Answer: Government spending is expected to play a crucial role in stimulating domestic demand, especially in the second half of the fiscal year. 

What are the implications of a delayed synchronous recovery in Western economies for India?
Answer: A delayed recovery in Western economies could affect global demand, potentially impacting India's export-driven sectors and overall economic growth. 

What measures can India take to mitigate the risks associated with geopolitical uncertainties and global trade disruptions?
Answer: India can focus on diversifying its export markets, strengthening domestic industries, and enhancing trade partnerships to reduce dependence on specific regions and mitigate associated risks.
